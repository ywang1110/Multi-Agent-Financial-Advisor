RETIREMENT PLANNING GUIDE
==========================

RETIREMENT SAVINGS VEHICLES (US)
----------------------------------
1. 401(k): Employer-sponsored plan. 2024 contribution limit: $23,000 ($30,500 if age 50+). Many employers offer matching—always contribute at least enough to capture the full match (free money).

2. Traditional IRA: Tax-deductible contributions (income limits apply). 2024 limit: $7,000 ($8,000 if 50+). Withdrawals taxed as ordinary income in retirement.

3. Roth IRA: After-tax contributions, tax-free growth and withdrawals. Best for those who expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement. 2024 income phase-out: $146,000-$161,000 (single), $230,000-$240,000 (married).

4. SEP-IRA / Solo 401(k): For self-employed individuals. Higher contribution limits.

THE 4% RULE
-----------
A widely used retirement withdrawal guideline: withdraw 4% of your portfolio in year one, then adjust for inflation each year. A $1M portfolio supports ~$40,000/year in withdrawals with high probability of lasting 30 years.

Required Retirement Nest Egg = Annual Expenses in Retirement × 25

SOCIAL SECURITY OPTIMIZATION
------------------------------
- Claiming at 62: Reduced benefits (up to 30% reduction).
- Claiming at 67 (Full Retirement Age): Full benefits.
- Delaying to 70: Benefits increase by 8% per year after FRA. Optimal strategy for those in good health with other income sources.

RETIREMENT INCOME SOURCES
--------------------------
1. Investment portfolio withdrawals
2. Social Security
3. Pension (if applicable)
4. Rental income
5. Part-time work / consulting

PLANNING MILESTONES BY AGE
---------------------------
Age 30s: Maximize 401(k) match, start Roth IRA, build emergency fund (3-6 months expenses).
Age 40s: Increase savings rate, consider life insurance, plan for college expenses if applicable.
Age 50s: Catch-up contributions, review asset allocation (shift toward conservative), estimate Social Security benefits.
Age 60s: Finalize withdrawal strategy, consider healthcare costs, evaluate annuities.

HEALTHCARE IN RETIREMENT
-------------------------
Medicare eligibility begins at 65. Gap coverage needed if retiring before 65 (COBRA, marketplace plans, or spouse coverage). Long-term care insurance should be considered at 50-60.
Average healthcare costs in retirement: $315,000 per couple (Fidelity 2023 estimate).
