INVESTMENT RISK MANAGEMENT
===========================

TYPES OF INVESTMENT RISK
-------------------------
1. Market Risk (Systematic): Affects all investments. Cannot be diversified away. Includes economic recessions, interest rate changes, geopolitical events.

2. Specific Risk (Unsystematic): Company or sector-specific. Can be reduced through diversification. Example: single stock concentration risk.

3. Inflation Risk: The risk that investment returns do not keep pace with inflation. Cash and low-yield bonds are most vulnerable.

4. Liquidity Risk: Inability to sell an investment quickly at fair value. Real estate and private equity carry higher liquidity risk.

5. Sequence of Returns Risk: Critical in early retirement—a market downturn in the first few years of withdrawals can permanently impair a portfolio.

DIVERSIFICATION STRATEGIES
---------------------------
- Across asset classes: Stocks + bonds + real estate + cash
- Across geographies: US + international developed + emerging markets
- Across sectors: Technology, healthcare, financials, consumer staples, energy, etc.
- Across time: Dollar-cost averaging (DCA) reduces timing risk

RISK METRICS
------------
- Standard Deviation: Measures volatility. Higher = more risk.
- Beta: Measures sensitivity to market movements. Beta > 1 means more volatile than market.
- Sharpe Ratio: Risk-adjusted return. Higher is better. Formula: (Return - Risk-Free Rate) / Standard Deviation.
- Maximum Drawdown: Largest peak-to-trough decline. Important for retirement portfolios.

HEDGING TECHNIQUES
------------------
- Bonds: Negative or low correlation with stocks during recessions.
- Gold / Commodities: Hedge against inflation and currency debasement.
- Options (Protective Puts): Insurance against sharp declines. Cost money but limit downside.
- International Diversification: Different economic cycles reduce concentration.

BEHAVIORAL RISK
---------------
The biggest risk for most investors is themselves. Common behavioral biases:
- Loss aversion: Fear of losses causes premature selling.
- Recency bias: Overweighting recent market performance.
- Overconfidence: Excessive trading leading to underperformance.
- Panic selling: Locking in losses during market corrections.

Solution: Have a written Investment Policy Statement (IPS) and automate contributions.

RISK CAPACITY vs RISK TOLERANCE
---------------------------------
Risk Tolerance: Psychological comfort with volatility (subjective).
Risk Capacity: Financial ability to absorb losses without impacting goals (objective).
Both must be considered. A young high-earner may have high capacity but low tolerance—and that is perfectly valid.
